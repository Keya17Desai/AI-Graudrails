NEXORA TECHNOLOGIES — CODE OF CONDUCT
Applicable to all employees, contractors, and interns
Version 1.4 | Effective January 2025

================================================================
SECTION 1: OUR VALUES
================================================================

Nexora Technologies operates on four core values:

1. INTEGRITY — We do what we say, say what we mean, and own our mistakes.
2. CUSTOMER OBSESSION — Every decision starts with the question: does this make our customers more successful?
3. SPEED WITH QUALITY — We move fast without cutting corners on reliability or security.
4. INCLUSION — We build a workplace where every person feels valued and heard.

================================================================
SECTION 2: RESPECTFUL WORKPLACE
================================================================

All employees are expected to treat every colleague, client, vendor, and visitor with dignity and respect. Nexora has zero tolerance for:

- Harassment of any kind (verbal, physical, sexual, or psychological)
- Discrimination based on gender, religion, caste, race, age, disability, sexual orientation, or any other protected characteristic
- Bullying, intimidation, or threats
- Creating a hostile work environment

Any employee who experiences or witnesses such behaviour should report it immediately to HR (hr@nexora.internal) or the anonymous ethics hotline (ethics.nexora.internal). All reports are treated confidentially.

Retaliation against anyone who makes a good-faith report is itself a violation of this code and grounds for disciplinary action.

================================================================
SECTION 3: CONFLICTS OF INTEREST
================================================================

A conflict of interest occurs when personal interests could compromise, or appear to compromise, professional judgment and actions.

Employees must disclose to HR:
- Any personal financial interest in a vendor, partner, or competitor
- A family member employed by a client, vendor, or competitor in a decision-making role
- Outside employment or consulting that overlaps with Nexora's business
- Any board membership or advisory role at another company

Disclosure does not automatically mean a conflict exists. HR and the employee's department head will review and determine appropriate steps.

================================================================
SECTION 4: CONFIDENTIALITY
================================================================

Employees have access to sensitive information — client data, product roadmaps, financials, employee records. This information is a business asset and a legal obligation to protect.

Employees must:
- Not share confidential information with anyone outside Nexora without explicit authorisation
- Not discuss client names, deals, or financials in public places
- Not post about clients, internal strategy, or unreleased products on social media
- Return or destroy all confidential information upon leaving the company

Confidentiality obligations survive employment. Employees who leave Nexora remain bound by the NDA signed at joining for a period of 2 years.

================================================================
SECTION 5: GIFTS AND ENTERTAINMENT
================================================================

Employees must not accept gifts exceeding ₹2,000 in value from any vendor, client, or partner. Gifts above this threshold must be reported to the Finance team and either returned or donated.

Business meals with clients and partners are acceptable when:
- The purpose is legitimate business discussion
- The cost is reasonable (guideline: under ₹5,000 per person)
- It is not part of an active procurement decision where the vendor is seeking to influence the outcome

Employees must not offer or accept anything that could be perceived as a bribe to influence a business decision.

================================================================
SECTION 6: SOCIAL MEDIA
================================================================

Employees are encouraged to be proud Nexora ambassadors on LinkedIn and other professional platforms. When posting about Nexora:

- Do not share unreleased product features, pricing, or roadmap details
- Do not disclose client names or client relationships without Marketing approval
- Do not make statements that could be construed as official Nexora positions unless authorised to do so
- Clearly indicate that personal views are your own and not Nexora's

================================================================
SECTION 7: COMPLIANCE WITH LAWS
================================================================

All employees must comply with applicable Indian laws and the laws of jurisdictions where Nexora operates. This includes:
- Information Technology Act, 2000
- Personal Data Protection Bill requirements
- Foreign Exchange Management Act (for international transactions)
- Competition Act, 2002 (no price-fixing, bid rigging, or anti-competitive conduct)

When in doubt, consult the Legal team (legal@nexora.internal).

================================================================
SECTION 8: DISCIPLINARY PROCESS
================================================================

Violations of this code will result in disciplinary action proportionate to the severity:

Level 1 (Minor): Verbal warning, documented by HR
Level 2 (Moderate): Written warning, placed in employee record
Level 3 (Serious): Final written warning or suspension
Level 4 (Gross Misconduct): Immediate termination

Examples of gross misconduct: harassment, theft, fraud, data breach caused by gross negligence, sharing confidential data with competitors.
